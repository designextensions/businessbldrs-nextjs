To recreate this design, you would aim for a "Bold Industrial Editorial" aesthetic. It blends the high-impact confidence of a modern marketing agency with the trustworthiness of an established consultancy.

Here is the comprehensive design specification:

1. Core Aesthetic: "High-Contrast Magazine"
The site feels less like a standard tech startup and more like a premium business publication. It relies on massive scale, stark contrast, and sophisticated typography to command authority.

Vibe: Confident, proven, authoritative, yet energetic.
Key Texture: Subtle "noise" or grain, vintage rubber stamps (approved, certified), and halftone patterns are used to add depth and history, preventing it from looking too "flat" or digital.
2. Color Palette
The scheme is strictly high-contrast, avoiding soft pastels or gradients in favor of solid blocks of color.

Primary Action (Yellow): #FACC15 (Vivid Yellow). Used for "shine" effects, accent lines, shadows, and hover states. It screams "attention" without being aggressive.
Secondary/Base (Dark Charcoal): #1A1918. Used for text, primary buttons, and heavy section backgrounds. It’s softer than pure black, giving a premium "print" feel.
Backgrounds: A mix of Pure White (#FFFFFF) and warm Stone greys (bg-stone-50, bg-stone-100). This warmth distinguishes it from the sterile cold greys of typical SaaS sites.
Functional Colors: Deep Red for "Problem" sections (highlighting pain points) to trigger psychological alertness.
3. Typography
The type hierarchy is the single most important design element.

Headlines (Display): Massive, heavy-weight Sans-Serif (likely Poppins or Inter Black). Often set in UPPERCASE with tight letter spacing (tracking-tighter). Font sizes are exaggerated (up to 7rem on desktop) to act as graphical elements themselves.
Body/Narrative (Serif): An elegant Serif font (like Lora or Playfair). Used for subtitles, quotes, and narrative text. This adds the "human" and "editorial" element, making the agency feel thoughtful and strategic rather than just transactional.
UI Elements: Bold, uppercase Sans-Serif with wide letter spacing (tracking-widest) for buttons, badges, and navigation labels.
4. Layout & Structure
Banding: The page is structured in alternating high-contrast horizontal bands (White → Dark → Yellow → Stone). This creates a strong rhythm that pulls the user down the page.
"Bento" Grids: Content is often contained in varying-sized boxes with thick borders (border-2) and sharp corners (rounded-sm), reminiscent of newspaper layouts or bento grids.
Hard Edges: Almost no rounded corners (using rounded-sm or rounded-none). This reinforces the "Business/Industrial" feel—it’s precise and engineered.
5. Interaction Design
"Tactile" Feel: Buttons and cards don't just fade; they have solid offset shadows (box-shadow: 4px 4px 0px 0px #000) that "press" down on hover (translating x/y). It feels mechanical and clicky.
Staggered Reveals: Content doesn't appear all at once. Grid items load with slight delays (0.1s, 0.2s, 0.3s) to guide the eye.
Sticky Elements: Functional sidebars (like in the FAQ) stick to the viewport while content scrolls, mimicking a table of contents.
6. Narrative Flow (The "StoryBrand" Structure)
The design isn't just visual; it follows a strict psychological script:

Hero: Big Promise ("Marketing That Works").
Stakes: The Problem ("Is marketing costing you money?").
Guide: Empathy & Authority (Trust badges, years of experience).
Plan: Simple Steps (1, 2, 3 Process).
Success: Social Proof (Video testimonials, case studies).
Call to Action: Clear, repeated imperative ("Request a Quote").
