 Critical Issues

  1. Title Tag Brand Duplication — All Service Pages

  Impact: High

  Multiple pages repeat "Business Builders" twice in the title tag:
  - /services → "Marketing Services | Business Builders | Business Builders"
  - /website-design → "Website Design & Development | Business Builders | Business Builders"
  - /seo-services → "SEO Services St. Augustine | Business Builders | Business Builders"

  This is almost certainly a CMS template bug where the brand is appended at both the page level and the global
  template level. Google may truncate or rewrite these titles, and it wastes the character budget.

  Fix: Audit your CMS title template — remove the global brand append, or remove it from individual page fields. Target
   format: "SEO Services St. Augustine | Business Builders" (≤60 chars).

  ---
  2. Canonical Tags — Not Confirmed Present

  Impact: High

  No canonical tags were detected on the homepage, /about, /services, or service pages. Without canonicals, Google has
  to guess which version of your URLs to treat as authoritative — especially risky with:
  - www vs. non-www
  - HTTP vs. HTTPS variations
  - Any URL parameters from campaigns or tracking

  Fix: Add <link rel="canonical" href="https://businessbldrs.com/[page]"> to every page's <head>. If you're on a modern
   CMS/framework, this is typically a one-line config.

  ---
  3. Sitemap Quality — 414 URLs, Bulk Timestamps

  Impact: Medium-High

  - All 414 URLs share the same timestamp (2026-03-02) — this is a bulk-regeneration artifact that reduces the
  sitemap's usefulness for communicating freshness to Google.
  - Priority values are inconsistent — conversion pages and homepage should be 1.0, service pages 0.8, articles 0.6,
  legal pages 0.3. Currently mixed.
  - No audit of status codes — at 414 URLs, there's risk of 301/404s in the sitemap. Google's guidance: sitemap should
  only include canonicalized, indexable 200 pages.

  Fix: Run all 414 URLs through Screaming Frog or a redirect checker. Remove any that return non-200 or have noindex.
  Set timestamps to actual last-modified dates from your CMS.
